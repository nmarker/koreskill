Questions?

How are lessons delivered?

We use a combination of Google classroom, MS Teams for delivering our lessons.

Why lean to code?

Whether to pursue a career in coding depends on one's interests, aptitudes, and career goals. Coding can be a rewarding career, offering high salaries, job security, and opportunities for creativity and problem-solving. However, it can also be demanding and require constant learning and adaptation. Researching the field and enrolling in Caltech coding Bootcamp can help with the decision.

What ages or grades is the program designed for?

Our programs are designed for students in grades 4–12, with age-appropriate curriculum across elementary, middle, and high school levels.

Does my child need prior coding experience?

Not at all! Our beginner courses are designed for students new to programming, while intermediate and advanced options are available for returning learners.

What technologies or languages are taught?

Students learn Python, HTML/CSS, Java, Scratch, and more—through real projects that build problem-solving and computational thinking.

How long are the classes?

Classes are typically 60–90 minutes each and run for 4 to 6 weeks, depending on the course. Sessions are live and fully interactive.

What is the class size?

Each class typically has 6–10 students to ensure every learner gets individual attention from the instructor.

What are the technical requirements for students?

Students will need a computer with internet access and a modern web browser. Some classes may also require installing a free code editor like VS Code.

Are the classes recorded?

Yes, most sessions are recorded so students can review materials later or catch up if they miss a class.

How do I know which course is right for my child?

You can contact us directly and we’ll help evaluate your child’s experience and interests to recommend a suitable course track.
